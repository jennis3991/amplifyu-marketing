My story — told in seven beats

Every story has
a turning point.

AmplifyU's Story Architect uses the Pixar framework — seven narrative beats that turn any experience into a story that lands. Here's mine.

Beat 01
Once Upon a Time…

I was working for one of the world's largest corporate companies — navigating high-stakes conversations, global stakeholders, and rooms where how you communicate is as important as what you say.

Beat 02
Every Day…

I engaged with global stakeholders, senior leaders, and some of the most talented communicators in the world. I watched what separated the ones who were heard from the ones who weren't.

Beat 03
One Day…

I became a mother of two small children. And everything changed. Time became the most precious thing I owned. Every meeting, every conversation, every sentence had to earn its place.

Beat 04
Because of That…

I realised that communication isn't just one skill among many. It's the skill that multiplies every other one. Clarity accelerates decisions. Stories build trust. Presence opens doors.

Beat 05
Because of That…

I looked for the tool I needed — science-backed, practically structured, designed for a real professional life. Something that fit inside fifteen minutes, not a week-long retreat. It didn't exist.

Beat 06
Until Finally…

I built it. AmplifyU — fourteen deliberate sessions, built from sixteen years of lived experience, distilled into the fifteen minutes a day that change the rest of them.

Beat 07
Ever Since Then…

My mission has been simple: help professionals communicate with clarity, confidence, and purpose — so they can work faster, influence more, and come home with something left to give.

Built from lived experience

Every module exists because I
needed it first.

AmplifyU wasn't built from a curriculum. It was built from the moments that shaped me — the high-stakes conversations, the visibility gaps, the pressure to be precise when everything else was pulling for my attention.

The lived experience

High-Stakes Conversations

Negotiating with global partners, presenting to boards, navigating conflict with senior stakeholders — daily. There was no room for unclear thinking.

Day 6 gives you the science, the scripts, and an AI simulation to prepare for real conversations before they happen. Disagreeing with leadership, delivering difficult feedback, holding a position under pressure.

Day 6 · Composure

Building Your Brand

In a large organisation, talent is never enough. The professionals who advance are the ones whose value is visible to the people who decide what happens next.

Days 10–11 cover the PIE model, the Halo Effect, and an AI-powered LinkedIn Brand Audit — giving you the strategy to close the gap between how you're seen and how you want to be known.

Days 10–11 · Presence & Brand
03

The lived experience

Empathy & Active Listening

Working across cultures, seniorities, and competing priorities taught me that listening — really listening — is rarer and more powerful than any speaking skill.

Day 5 · Connection

The most influential people in any room aren't the loudest. They're the ones who make others feel heard — and then move the whole room with a single, well-placed sentence. Day 5 builds that skill from the ground up: Narrative Transportation, emotional resonance, and the listening that makes your words land harder.

04

The lived experience

Storytelling That Drives Outcomes

Every successful pitch, budget request, and career conversation I've been part of had a story at its centre. Data alone has never moved a room.

Days 3–4 teach the SAR and Pixar frameworks — turning your experiences into stories that get remembered, respected, and acted on. Including the Story Architect tool used to build the story above.

Days 3–4 · Storytelling

The lived experience

Visibility & Exposure

I watched brilliant people be overlooked — not because they lacked skill, but because their value wasn't seen by the people who needed to see it.

Day 13 builds your exposure strategy: Weak Tie Theory, Sponsorship vs Mentorship, the Opportunity Map, and the Promotion Room simulation — revealing what leaders might be saying and building your plan to change it.
